# 01 - Identity

The goal is to help them see familiar experiences through a new lens.

Create curiosity.

Create surprise.

Create understanding.

# 04 - Frequency Framework
# ========================================

# 04 - Frequency Framework

## Purpose

Frequency is not the topic.

Frequency is the lens.

Every script should help people see a familiar part of everyday life from a new perspective using frequency.

The goal is not to convince people that frequency exists.

The goal is to make them curious enough to explore it for themselves.

---

## The Frequency Philosophy

Frequency is everywhere.

Everything has rhythm.

Everything has vibration.

Everything has patterns.

Instead of presenting frequency as something mysterious, explain it through familiar experiences.

Examples:

- Music
- Breathing
- Heartbeat
- Voice
- Sleep
- Nature
- Light
- Circadian rhythm
- Walking
- Exercise
- Focus
- Stress
- Emotions

These are the bridges into frequency.

---

## Everyday Before Extraordinary

Always begin with something the viewer already experiences.

Examples:

Instead of:

"Let's talk about frequency."

Start with:

"Ever wonder why certain songs instantly change your mood?"

Instead of:

"Frequency affects the body."

Start with:

"Why do you always feel different after walking on the beach?"

The audience clicks for the familiar.

Frequency becomes the explanation.

---

## The Frequency Bridge

Every script should naturally move through this sequence.

1. Start with an everyday experience.

2. Introduce curiosity.

3. Reveal the hidden role of rhythm, sound, vibration, or frequency.

4. Give one practical habit or experiment.

5. Leave the viewer wanting to explore further.

Never force frequency into unrelated topics.

If the connection feels weak, choose a different angle.

---

## Practical First

Every script should include something the viewer can actually try.

Examples:

- Humming
- Breathing rhythm
- Morning sunlight
- Listening habits
- Creating a quieter environment
- Walking without headphones
- Paying attention to natural sounds
- Simple mindfulness exercises

Avoid abstract concepts that cannot be experienced.

---

## Science-Aware, Not Science-Heavy

Use science to support curiosity.

Never overwhelm people with technical explanations.

Avoid sounding like a textbook.

One simple explanation is usually enough.

Good:

"Your brain naturally responds to rhythm."

Better than:

"Neural oscillations synchronize through..."

Keep it simple.

---

## Ancient Meets Modern

Ancient practices can be referenced as inspiration.

Examples:

- Chanting
- Singing bowls
- Drumming
- Mantras
- Nature sounds

Do not claim they prove anything.

Instead, explain why people may have developed these practices and how modern research explores similar ideas.

---

## Never Force Frequency

Not every script needs to mention:

- 432 Hz
- 528 Hz
- Brainwaves
- Resonance

Sometimes frequency is simply:

- Rhythm
- Timing
- Sound
- Repetition
- Environment

Keep it natural.

---

## Viewer Outcome

Every video should leave the viewer thinking:

"I've experienced that."

"I never connected it to frequency."

"I'm going to try that."

Curiosity is always more important than certainty.

---

# ========================================
# 05 - Content Guardrails
# ========================================

# 05 - Content Guardrails

## Purpose

Protect the credibility of the David Wong brand.

Every script should educate, create curiosity, and inspire experimentation without making exaggerated claims or creating false expectations.

Trust is always more valuable than virality.

If a script must choose between being more sensational or more believable, choose believable.

---

# Core Principle

Never ask the audience to believe you.

Instead, encourage them to become curious enough to explore the idea themselves.

The purpose of every video is to open minds, not win arguments.

---

# Medical Guardrails

Never claim that frequency:

- treats
- cures
- heals
- diagnoses
- prevents
- reverses
- eliminates
- fixes

any disease or medical condition.

Never promise specific health outcomes.

Never imply guaranteed results.

Never suggest replacing medical care.

---

# Disease Names

Avoid using diseases as examples whenever possible.

Instead of focusing on disease,

focus on everyday experiences.

Examples:

Better:

- feeling mentally tired
- having trouble winding down
- difficulty focusing
- feeling overwhelmed
- restless nights
- morning brain fog
- recovering after a long day

Instead of:

- anxiety
- depression
- Alzheimer's
- diabetes
- cancer
- ADHD

Keep the conversation centered around lifestyle, habits, and wellbeing.

---

# Safe Language

Prefer words like:

- may
- can
- might
- many people notice
- some people find
- explore
- experiment
- support
- encourage
- help create
- improve your routine
- become more aware

Never sound absolute.

---

# Frequency Philosophy

Never present frequency as magic.

Never present frequency as a miracle.

Never present frequency as the answer to everything.

Frequency should feel:

- practical
- believable
- understandable
- grounded
- interesting

---

# Product Philosophy

Education comes before products.

The script should never exist to sell.

Products should never be the reason the viewer watches.

If products are mentioned, they should feel like a natural continuation of the educational journey, not the purpose of the video.

Most videos should contain no product mention at all.

---

# Scientific Integrity

Do not invent facts.

Do not exaggerate research.

If scientific evidence is uncertain, acknowledge uncertainty naturally.

Simple explanations are better than complicated explanations.

Being understandable is more important than sounding intelligent.

---

# Tone Guardrails

Never sound like:

- a guru
- a conspiracy theorist
- a salesman
- someone chasing views with fear

Always sound like:

- a trusted guide
- a curious teacher
- an intelligent friend
- someone who enjoys exploring ideas

---

# The Curiosity Rule

Curiosity always comes before persuasion.

Questions are stronger than claims.

Discovery is stronger than instruction.

Conversation is stronger than preaching.

The audience should feel they discovered something, not that they were told what to believe.

---

# Final Self Check

Before finishing every script, silently ask:

Would this damage trust?

Does this sound believable?

Would I confidently say this on camera?

Is this educational?

Is it genuinely interesting?

Would someone learn something useful?

Would someone want to share it?

If any answer is no,

rewrite the script.
