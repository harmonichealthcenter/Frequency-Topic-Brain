# 03 - Viral Psychology

## The Mission

People do not watch videos because they are educational.

People watch videos because they are emotionally irresistible.

Education is the reward.

Curiosity is the entrance fee.

---

## Rule 1

Never start with frequency.

Start with something people already experience.

Examples:

Coffee

Sleep

Stress

Rice

Walking

Music

Phones

Headaches

Noise

Dreams

Exercise

Morning routines

Then reveal frequency as the hidden explanation.

---

## Rule 2

The first sentence exists for only one purpose.

Make the viewer NEED the second sentence.

Nothing else.

---

## Rule 3

Every sentence must earn the next sentence.

Ask before writing:

Why would they keep watching?

If the answer is weak,

rewrite.

---

## Rule 4

The biggest attention triggers

- Contradiction
- Curiosity
- Hidden knowledge
- Identity
- Surprise
- Recognition
- Practical benefit
- Mystery

Every script should trigger at least two.

---

## Rule 5

The audience loves discovering they were wrong.

Examples:

Most people...

Actually...

Everyone thinks...

But...

You've probably been told...

Here's what almost nobody explains...

---

## Rule 6

People love familiar topics.

Never start with obscure science.

Instead start with:

Sleep

Coffee

Walking

Stress

Noise

Focus

Dreams

Phone habits

Food

Breathing

Music

Then connect them to frequency.

---

## Rule 7

Reward curiosity quickly.

Never make viewers wait 30 seconds.

Reward them.

Then immediately create another curiosity gap.

---

## Rule 8

One sentence.

One job.

Sentence 1

Stop scrolling.

Sentence 2

Increase curiosity.

Sentence 3

Reveal.

Sentence 4

Create another question.

Sentence 5

Reveal.

Sentence 6

Practical takeaway.

Sentence 7

Memorable insight.

Sentence 8

Conversation CTA.

---

## Rule 9

The audience does not want information.

They want transformation.

Every script should make them think:

"I've never thought about it like that."

---

## Rule 10

Never sound educational.

Sound fascinating.

Never lecture.

Discover together.

The viewer should feel like they figured it out with you.

---

## Rule 11

Questions outperform explanations.

Whenever possible,

turn explanations into questions.

Examples:

Why do some sounds instantly calm you?

What if coffee isn't the reason you feel awake?

What happens if your environment has its own frequency?

---

## Rule 12

Every script should answer one question only.

One problem.

One idea.

One solution.

Never overload the viewer.

---

## Rule 13

Always finish with possibility.

The ending should make people think:

"I should try that."

"I need part two."

"I wonder what else I don't know."

That feeling creates comments.
